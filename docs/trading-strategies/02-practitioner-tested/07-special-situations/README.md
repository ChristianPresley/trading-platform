# 07 — Special Situations

Strategies that exploit specific corporate events, market anomalies, or structural inefficiencies outside the standard technical/fundamental analysis framework. These approaches target niche opportunities that arise from information asymmetries, behavioral biases, or institutional constraints.

## Key Themes

- **Event Dependence**: All strategies in this section are event-driven, generating signals only when specific conditions or announcements occur. Signal frequency ranges from daily (earnings announcements) to sporadic (distressed debt opportunities during credit crises).
- **Expertise Intensity**: Several strategies (M&A, distressed debt) require deep domain expertise beyond quantitative analysis — legal knowledge, corporate finance, and sometimes operational turnaround skills.
- **Limited Crypto Applicability**: Special situations strategies are predominantly equity-market phenomena. Only distressed debt has a conceptual (though structurally different) crypto analogue.

## Overall Assessment

Special situations strategies represent the most heterogeneous category, spanning from highly quantitative (short interest, earnings premium) to deeply qualitative (M&A, distressed debt). The strongest strategies — M&A arbitrage and distressed debt — also require the most expertise and infrastructure. The most quantitative strategies — short interest and earnings premium — have the weakest risk-adjusted returns, likely reflecting the ease with which these signals can be replicated and arbitraged.

Soccer clubs' arbitrage is an academic curiosity with interesting behavioral finance implications but severe practical limitations that prevent meaningful deployment.

For this trading platform, the earnings announcement premium offers the most systematic, automatable opportunity from this category, despite its modest Sharpe ratio. M&A and distressed debt are better suited to dedicated funds with specialized expertise and infrastructure.
