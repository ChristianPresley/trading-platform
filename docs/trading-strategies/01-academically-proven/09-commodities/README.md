# 09 - Commodity Strategies

Strategies exploiting risk premia, structural features, and behavioral patterns in commodity futures markets. Commodity markets differ fundamentally from equities due to the physical nature of the underlying assets, the presence of commercial hedgers, and the term structure of futures contracts.

## Key Themes

- **Risk transfer premia**: Hedgers (producers and consumers) pay speculators a premium for bearing price risk, creating structural long-term returns in commodity futures
- **Term structure exploitation**: The shape of the futures curve (backwardation vs. contango) is a powerful predictor of cross-sectional commodity returns
- **Momentum persistence**: Cross-sectional momentum in commodities is well-documented but weaker and more volatile than equity momentum
- **Higher-moment effects**: Skewness preferences among investors create cross-sectional return predictability

## General Considerations

Commodity futures strategies require understanding of contract roll mechanics, margin requirements, and the distinction between spot returns, roll returns, and collateral returns. Most documented returns are for fully collateralized positions. Transaction costs in commodity futures are generally low, but liquidity varies significantly across contracts. The crypto-applicable strategies in this section relate to perpetual futures funding rates, which share structural similarities with commodity roll yields.
