# Overnight and Swing Trading Strategies

Strategies that exploit overnight return anomalies or hold positions across multiple trading sessions. These target the well-documented phenomenon that most equity market returns accrue overnight (close-to-open) rather than intraday (open-to-close).

## Common Themes

- **Overnight edge is real**: Empirical evidence shows that virtually all S&P 500 returns since 1993 have come from overnight holds (close-to-open), with intraday (open-to-close) contributing zero cumulative return.
- **Low time-in-market**: Overnight strategies are invested roughly 8-15% of the time, dramatically reducing exposure to tail risk events during trading hours.
- **Crisis performance**: Many overnight strategies performed exceptionally well during the 2008 financial crisis and 2020 COVID crash.
- **Crypto advantage**: 24/7 crypto markets eliminate the traditional close/open boundary, but the concept adapts by using equity session boundaries as reference points.
- **Transaction costs**: Frequent daily round-trips require attention to commissions, slippage, and short-selling costs.
